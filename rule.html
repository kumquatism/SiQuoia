Mention this (from SRS)...

At first glance, SiQuoia may look like an ordinary quiz program. On a generic scheme, SiQuoia is basically a multiple choice type of quiz program with each set consisting of one question followed by four answers with one and only one of them being correct. In order to participate, users/gamers register with SeQuoia, paying a small registration fee. They could also register to play a sample game but only paid users/gamers can continue playing beyond the sample set. Each game session begins with login (or registration). As players progress through levels, achievements are tracked and points awarded. Points accumulated can be later exchanged for rewards such as quiz packets and game memorabilia. Gamers can submit questions (each with four relevant answers of which one and only one is correct) from within the game. Questions submitted will be verified, edited and categorized (Topic, subtopics) with in the system. Gamers earn points for questions submitted too. (Crowd Sourcing?) Gamers also earn points for introducing new gamers that register and play beyond the sample game. Games are made available in question packets that can be purchased with either cash or reward points. A game can include multimedia content. Special game sets can be branded with corporate logos and be made available with codes.
